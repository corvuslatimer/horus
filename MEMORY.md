US-Israeli strikes on Iran, launched on 28 February 2026, have escalated into open conflict, with Iranian retaliatory missile attacks on Israel and US bases across the region. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the initial strikes, triggering uncertainty over regime stability and possible internal power struggles involving the Revolutionary Guards. The fighting risks drawing in Gulf states and proxies, raising the spectre of a wider regional war.

In Ukraine, Russian forces continue incremental advances around Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka and have seized additional villages in the east, while Ukrainian units have pushed forward near Dobropillya. Moscow launched a record 288 missiles in February, primarily targeting energy infrastructure, as winter fighting persisted at high intensity. Peace talks mediated by the US remain stalled, with Russia insisting on Ukrainian withdrawal from Donetsk as a precondition.

US-China relations remain tense but managed, with President Trump scheduled to visit Beijing at the end of March for talks focused on tariffs and trade truce extension. The Supreme Court’s recent reversal of certain China-focused tariffs has eased immediate escalation risks, while the 2026 National Defense Strategy reaffirms deterrence in the Indo-Pacific, prioritising Taiwan and allied burden-sharing without signalling imminent confrontation.

Carriers have halted all transits through the Strait of Hormuz and suspended Suez/Red Sea routings following the Iran conflict, trapping roughly 450,000 TEU inside the Gulf. Houthi forces have signalled renewed attacks in the Red Sea in solidarity with Iran, compounding earlier disruptions. Roughly 20% of global oil supply and significant LNG flows remain at direct risk from closure or harassment at these chokepoints.

Brent crude has surged above $82 per barrel on the Hormuz crisis, embedding a substantial geopolitical risk premium that markets expect to persist. Short-term volatility will dominate energy, shipping, and inflation-sensitive assets over the next 1–2 weeks, with potential for further spikes if Iranian retaliation widens or if Gulf production facilities are hit, while broader equities face renewed downside pressure from uncertainty.
